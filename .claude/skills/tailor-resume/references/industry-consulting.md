# Industry: Consulting

Applies to: strategy consulting (McKinsey, BCG, Bain), management / Big-4 (Deloitte, EY, KPMG, PwC), boutique (Oliver Wyman, Kearney, LEK, Parthenon, Roland Berger), internal strategy / corporate-development roles.

Consulting resumes test for the same structure the firms use in case interviews: **clear problem, structured approach, quantified result**. The "MECE" mindset is visible in formatting.

## Length
- **Undergrad / MBA applicant:** 1 page, strict.
- **Experienced hire / partner track:** 1–2 pages, preferably 1.
- Multi-page consulting resumes are read as unstructured.

## Order of sections
1. Header (no photo except DE/CH/AT)
2. Education (MBAs with top-school brand: lead; include GMAT ≥720 or GRE ≥330 if recent)
3. Experience
4. Leadership / Extracurriculars (**critical** for MBA and campus hires — clubs, presidencies, athletics, nonprofits)
5. Skills & Certifications (languages, programming if applicable, sector certifications)
6. Additional (interests, publications/patents if any)

Note: "Leadership" as its own section is a consulting-specific convention — firms weight it heavily as a proxy for client presence.

## Bullet style: PAR / CAR

Every bullet follows **Problem → Action → Result** (or Challenge → Action → Result). Two-to-three lines max.

```
Advised top-3 global pharma on post-merger integration of a €6B acquisition;
structured 14 workstreams across 4 markets, led diligence on commercial
synergies, and built the integration playbook — delivered €180M run-rate
savings in Year 1 (vs. €120M target).
```

Break rules if helpful:
- Start with the **client archetype** ("top-3 European bank", "Fortune-50 retailer"), not the case name.
- Include **engagement size** ($M, team size, duration) where possible.
- Finish with **outcome**: $ saved / $ earned / % share / launch shipped / hire made.

## Quantification beats adjectives

Consulting reviewers have seen "complex, cross-functional stakeholder management" a million times. They want numbers:

| Weak                                      | Strong                                                                          |
|-------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Led strategy workstream                   | Led 5-person strategy workstream advising CEO and 3 SVPs                        |
| Drove significant cost savings            | Identified $42M annual cost savings (8% of SG&A) in procurement redesign        |
| Managed complex stakeholder alignment     | Aligned 12 country GMs on unified pricing framework across 22 markets           |

## MBA applicant specifics (for those targeting MBB / M7 MBAs)

- **Leadership section is equal in weight to Experience.** Treat club presidencies and varsity captaincies with the same rigor as work bullets.
- **Impact > activity**: "Grew club membership 120 → 340 (3×)" beats "Organized weekly meetings".
- **Quantify extracurriculars** the same way: dollars raised, events scaled, teams led.

## Keywords & phrasing
Firms scan for: *hypothesis-driven, structured, data-driven, first principles, top-down, bottom-up, recommendation, C-suite, executive presence, synthesized, triangulated, 80/20, MECE, go/no-go, commercial due diligence, operational due diligence, post-merger integration, zero-based budgeting, value creation plan, benchmarking, TAM/SAM/SOM, cost transformation, digital transformation*.

Use these honestly — don't paper over individual-contributor work with leadership language you didn't earn.

## Confidentiality
Client names are **almost always confidential**. Use:
- *"Top-3 European telco"*, *"Fortune-100 industrial conglomerate"*, *"Mid-market PE-backed SaaS business"*.
- Never disclose a named client unless the engagement was public.

## Format discipline
- Crisp sans-serif OR traditional serif — firms accept both. Avoid anything ornamental.
- 10–11pt, 0.75"–1" margins, black ink only.
- Right-aligned dates, bolded employer.
- **No** photos, DOB, marital status (US/UK). DACH / FR / JP follow local norm.
- Single-column. Do not use infographics or pie charts.

## GPA / test scores
- **US / MBA**: include GPA if ≥ 3.5 and degree is recent (≤7 years). Include GMAT (700+) or GRE (320+) if recent and high.
- **UK**: "First Class Honours" or "2:1" — plus university prize names.
- **India**: include percentage/CGPA; list IIT JEE / CAT rank if exceptional.
- Undergrad candidates: add SAT/ACT only if >98th percentile AND within 5 years of application.

## What to cut
- High-school anything (unless from an internationally-recognized institution relevant to the role)
- Every single course you took — list at most 4 "relevant coursework" lines
- Soft-skill adjectives not backed by examples

## Red flags
- Consulting-style phrasing on an obvious IC technical resume ("led the synthesis" for a solo coding project) — reviewers notice the seam.
- Leadership section filled with passive participation ("member of…")
- "Fluent" in five languages at B1 — specify CEFR or ILR levels.
