# Industry: Legal

Applies to: associate / counsel / partner roles at law firms, in-house counsel, paralegal, compliance officer, judicial clerkships, government attorneys.

Legal hiring is **credential-first, deal-sheet second**. Reviewers scan for law school, bar admission, and matter-level experience.

## Length
- **1L / 2L summer applicants:** 1 page, strict.
- **Junior associates (1–5 years):** 1 page.
- **Senior associates / counsel:** 1–2 pages.
- **Partners:** 2 pages + separate representative-matters sheet if requested.

## Order of sections
1. Header (no photo, no DOB US/UK)
2. Education (law school + undergrad; with law review, moot court, journal positions, honors)
3. Bar admissions & Licenses (state(s), year, registration numbers as customary)
4. Experience (with representative matters under each firm/role)
5. Clerkships (called out prominently if any — federal > state > appellate > trial)
6. Publications (law review articles, bar journal pieces)
7. Speaking engagements
8. Professional associations & activities (ABA committees, bar sections)
9. Pro bono / community involvement
10. Languages

## Education formatting
> *Harvard Law School, J.D., 2024. Editor, Harvard Law Review. Dean's Scholar Award in Contracts.*
> *Yale University, B.A. in Economics, summa cum laude, 2021. Phi Beta Kappa.*

Include class rank if strong (top 10%, top 5%), Order of the Coif, honors, journal positions, moot-court placements.

## Bar admissions line
> *Admitted: New York (2024, #1234567). Pending: California (July 2026 exam).*

## Matter-level experience
Under each firm, list representative matters — analog of IB's deal sheet:

```
Sullivan & Cromwell LLP, New York                              2024 – Present
Associate, M&A / Private Equity Group

  Representative transactions:
  • Represented [Fortune-50 industrial] in its $4.8B acquisition of
    [Target] — drafted MTA and ancillary agreements; managed 40+
    attorney cross-border team.
  • Advised sponsor on $900M LBO of [Target] — led NDA, LOI, diligence
    workstream; closed in 6 weeks.
  • Drafted and negotiated 14 commercial contracts for tech client
    launching in 6 EU markets (GDPR and EU DSM considerations).
```

For litigators:
> *Second-chaired 3-week federal jury trial (SDNY); cross-examined 4 fact witnesses and 1 expert.*
> *Authored successful motion to dismiss in $120M securities class action.*

## Practice-area keywords
- **Corporate / M&A**: definitive agreement, purchase agreement, disclosure schedules, CIM, go-shop, no-shop, reps & warranties, indemnification, MAE, earn-out
- **Capital markets**: S-1, S-3, F-1, 10b-5, Rule 144A, Reg S, underwriting agreement, comfort letter, due diligence
- **Litigation**: motion to dismiss, summary judgment, discovery, deposition, trial, appeal, opinion, brief
- **IP**: prosecution, PTAB, ITC, 101/102/103/112, patent family, licensing, FTO
- **Regulatory**: FCPA, sanctions (OFAC), export controls (ITAR/EAR), antitrust (HSR, EU merger), securities (SEC), banking (OCC, FRB, FDIC)
- **Employment**: Title VII, ADA, FLSA, ERISA, NLRA, works councils, transfer of undertakings
- **Compliance**: AML, KYC, sanctions screening, training, audit remediation

## Clerkships
Lead with the judge's name and court — this is the primary signal:
> *Law Clerk to the Hon. [Judge Name], U.S. Court of Appeals for the [Nth] Circuit, 2023–2024.*

## Confidentiality
Client names are usually confidential unless the matter is public. Use:
- *"Global pharmaceutical company"*, *"NASDAQ-listed fintech"*, *"Sovereign wealth fund"*.
- Filed cases with public captions may be named (e.g., *In re XYZ Securities Litig., 1:23-cv-00001 (S.D.N.Y.)*).

## Format discipline
- Traditional serif (Times, Garamond).
- Right-align dates, bold firm/court name.
- Dense but readable — no colour, no icons, single column.
- "Esq." never appears on your own name.

## Red flags
- Missing bar-admission line
- Listing law-review "member" as if it's an editor role
- Inflating first-chair status on trials
- Client names where NDAs apply
