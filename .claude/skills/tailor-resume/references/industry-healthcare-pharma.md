# Industry: Healthcare / Pharma / Biotech / Medical Device

Applies to: clinicians (MD, RN, NP, PA), clinical research (CRA, clinical-trial management, medical monitor, medical affairs), regulatory affairs, pharmacovigilance, medical writing, pharma R&D, biotech science, med-device engineering, hospital administration, healthcare consulting.

Hiring in this industry is **credential-heavy and compliance-sensitive**. Licensure, certifications, therapeutic area, and regulatory familiarity are table stakes.

## Resume vs. CV
- **US clinical & pharma roles:** traditional résumé, 1–2 pages. A **CV** (multi-page, full history) is sometimes requested and is the norm for academic medicine. When in doubt, produce a résumé for the job application and offer to produce a separate CV.
- **EU / UK / academic medical centres:** CV is the default; 2–6 pages.

## Length
- Clinician / clinical-research associate: 1–2 pages résumé; 4+ pages CV if asked.
- Regulatory / MSL / medical affairs: 2 pages.
- Pharma R&D scientist: CV style, 2–4 pages, includes publications.
- MD with research: full CV — 6+ pages acceptable.

## Order of sections (résumé)
1. Header
2. Professional summary (3–4 lines; therapeutic area and years-in-industry up front)
3. **Licensure & Certifications** — date awarded, state/country, number if customary (MD license number usually omitted; nursing license state + expiry kept)
4. Experience (clinical hours for clinicians; therapeutic area + phase for CRA/CRM)
5. Education & Training (including residency/fellowship, internships, rotations)
6. Publications / Posters / Presentations (selective for résumé; full for CV)
7. Professional Memberships (ASCO, AMA, ASH, ACS, RAPS, DIA, etc.)
8. Languages (fluency level matters in pharma — multinational trial work)

## Credentials to surface early

Always include — do not bury:
- **MD / DO / PhD / PharmD / MBBS / MBChB / MSc / RN / BSN / MSN / DNP / PA-C**
- **Board certifications** (ABIM, ABS, ABOS, ABP, etc.) with board name and year
- **Licensure**: state/country, number (RN), active status, expiry
- **Specialty certifications**: ACLS, PALS, BLS, CCRN, RAC, CCRP, PMP (for ops)
- **Regulatory qualifications**: RAC (US/EU/Global), CQA, CQE
- **Clinical research**: ACRP-CP, SOCRA CCRP, GCP-trained

## Bullet style
Three patterns depending on role:

### Clinician
> *Managed inpatient census of 18–22 patients on internal-medicine service; led daily rounds; supervised 2 interns and 3 medical students.*
> *First-assist on 140+ open and laparoscopic cholecystectomies during PGY-3.*

### Clinical research (CRA / CRM / medical monitor)
> *Monitored 14 sites across a Phase III oncology trial (NCT0XXXXXXX), n=320 enrolled; conducted 62 on-site and 40 remote visits; closed 2 sites for non-compliance without delay to data-lock.*
> *Authored protocol amendments 3 and 4 for a Phase II NASH study; shepherded IRB/EC approvals across 7 countries.*

### Pharma R&D / biotech scientist
> *Designed and executed PK/PD studies for a novel GLP-1 agonist in rodents and NHPs; findings supported IND filing accepted by FDA in Aug 2024.*
> *Developed LC-MS/MS bioanalytical method for a peptide therapeutic; achieved LLOQ 0.5 ng/mL in human plasma, validated to FDA bioanalytical guidance.*

### Regulatory affairs
> *Led FDA IND submission (eCTD) for a first-in-human oncology Phase I; no clinical hold. Primary liaison to OND review team across 3 Type-C meetings.*
> *Filed 12 CE marking technical files (Class IIa/IIb) under MDR; zero NCs raised by notified body.*

## Keywords by sub-sector

- **Clinical research**: ICH-GCP, FDA 21 CFR 11, CDISC, CTMS, EDC, Medidata Rave, Veeva, SDTM, ADaM, TMF, IRB/IEC, IND, IDE, CTA, site initiation visit, monitoring visit, close-out visit, enrollment, screen failure, protocol deviation, SAE reporting
- **Regulatory**: IND, NDA, BLA, 510(k), PMA, De Novo, MAA, CTA, orphan drug designation, breakthrough therapy, fast track, CE mark, MDR, IVDR, PMDA, TGA, HC, ANVISA
- **Pharmacovigilance**: ICSR, PSUR/PBRER, PADER, MedDRA coding, signal detection, risk-benefit, RMP/REMS, EudraVigilance, E2B(R3)
- **Medical affairs**: MSL, KOL, advisory board, HEOR, RWE, payer, value dossier, formulary, investigator-initiated trial (IIT)
- **Biotech R&D**: GLP, CMC, bioanalytical, DMPK, ADME, IND-enabling, NHP, in vivo, in vitro, CRISPR, mRNA, LNP, AAV, antibody, bispecific, ADC, cell therapy, gene therapy

## Therapeutic-area emphasis
State therapeutic areas explicitly in summary and throughout bullets: *oncology (solid tumors, heme)*, *immunology (RA, IBD)*, *cardiovascular*, *neurology*, *rare disease*, *infectious disease*, *metabolic/NASH*, *ophthalmology*, *dermatology*. Match the posting's stated area.

## Publications (CV style)
List in reverse chronological order; include PMID or DOI. Group as:
- Peer-reviewed articles
- Book chapters
- Posters & abstracts (ASCO, ASH, AHA, SFN, AACR, ACR — name the venue)
- Invited talks

## Confidentiality & compliance
- **Never list named investigators' personal info**, patient identifiers, or unblinded trial results from confidential sources.
- Trial identifiers (ClinicalTrials.gov NCT numbers) are public and **fine** to include.
- Unpublished / ongoing trial details: describe in aggregate ("Phase III oncology trial, n≈300, 2L NSCLC") without identifying the specific program if confidential.

## Red flags
- Missing licensure line for clinicians
- Therapeutic area absent from pharma résumés
- Using non-standard abbreviations the reviewer may not know — spell out on first use
- Mixing clinical hours claims with administrative time without distinction
- Claiming "GCP trained" without dates or provider (NIH, CITI, TransCelerate)
