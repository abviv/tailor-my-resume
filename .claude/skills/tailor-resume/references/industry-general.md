# Industry: General / Fallback

Use when the posting doesn't fit tech, banking/finance, consulting, healthcare/pharma, academia, or legal — or when the user explicitly asks for "generic professional" styling. Applies to product management (non-tech), operations, HR, marketing, sales, supply chain, K-12/higher-ed administration, nonprofit, government (non-legal), and most mid-market corporate roles.

This reference is also the **skeleton** the skill uses to scaffold a new industry reference when asked.

## Length
- Entry-level: 1 page.
- Mid-career (5–15 yrs): 1–2 pages.
- Senior / executive (15+ yrs): 2 pages, occasionally 3 for C-suite with diverse history.

## Order of sections
1. Header (contact info, LinkedIn; no photo US/UK)
2. Summary (3–4 lines; role type + years-of-experience + 2–3 standout strengths + target function)
3. Experience (reverse chronological)
4. Education
5. Certifications (PMP, Six Sigma, SHRM, Google Ads, HubSpot, etc.)
6. Skills (tools, methodologies, languages)
7. Awards (only if notable)
8. Volunteer / Community (if role-relevant)

## Bullet style: CAR (Challenge → Action → Result)

Every bullet should answer: *what was the situation, what did you do, what happened?*

> *Redesigned onboarding flow for a 2,400-store retail chain; cut first-week training time from 6 days to 3.5 days; est. $4.8M/yr savings in trainer hours.*
> *Launched B2B email program from zero; grew MQL volume 0 → 820/month in 7 months at $12 CAC, feeding a $7M pipeline.*

Rules:
- Past tense for past roles, present tense only for current.
- Numbers where possible — dollars, percentages, counts, time saved, people managed.
- Lead with the verb. Don't start bullets with "Responsible for" or "Duties included".

## Summary

Three to four lines. Structure:
1. **Who you are** (role + years): *"Supply-chain operations leader with 12 years of experience in CPG and e-commerce."*
2. **What you do** (signature strengths): *"Specialist in S&OP implementation, demand planning, and multi-node distribution network design."*
3. **What you've delivered** (headline result or two): *"Led $40M+ cost-takeout programs across 3 Fortune-500 supply chains; built teams of up to 35."*
4. **What you're looking for** (optional, only if motivation-to-move signals are needed).

## Keywords

Always pull the posting's **exact** phrasing for:
- Function / title keywords (e.g., "demand planner", "customer success manager")
- Tools (Salesforce, HubSpot, NetSuite, SAP, Workday, Tableau, PowerBI, Oracle, Jira, Asana)
- Methodologies (Six Sigma, Lean, Agile, Scrum, Kaizen, OKRs)
- Certifications (PMP, CSM, SHRM-CP, CFA if relevant, CPA, Six Sigma Green/Black Belt)

Mirror the posting — if the posting says "team leadership" rather than "management", echo that.

## Cultural and regional notes
- **US**: 1–2 pages, no photo, no DOB, no marital status. Punchy action verbs.
- **UK / IE**: "CV" conventionally, 2 pages, references line at bottom OK.
- **DE / AT / CH**: Lebenslauf with photo + DOB + place of birth + nationality + signature is traditional; many industries still expect it. Include a "Table of contents" or "Anlagen" only if sending a full Bewerbungsmappe.
- **FR**: 1 page strict; photo common; state "Titulaire du permis B" for roles involving driving.
- **JP**: Use Rirekisho (履歴書) + Shokumu-keirekisho (職務経歴書) format — not a Western résumé. Photo required.
- **India**: 2–3 pages acceptable; include date of birth, marital status, and parent's name (older convention, fading in tech).

## Format discipline
- Sans-serif (Calibri, Helvetica, Arial) or serif (Georgia, Cambria) — both OK.
- 10–11pt body, 0.75"–1" margins.
- Black ink; one accent colour OK for headings (navy, burgundy) but avoid rainbow.
- Single column for ATS safety; two columns only for roles without ATS screening (some creative, C-suite, academic).

## What to cut
- "References available upon request" — assumed; takes up space.
- Irrelevant hobbies, unless the role explicitly values them (e.g., coaching youth sports for a teaching role).
- Out-of-date tools the industry has moved past.

## Red flags
- Gaps > 6 months unexplained
- Inflated titles ("Chief Hustler", "Sales Ninja") — reviewers discount these
- Five-year-old bullets written in present tense
- Skill lists longer than the experience can support
